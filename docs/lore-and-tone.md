**The Unbound — World Lore & Tone Reference**

The world of *The Unbound* is a torus — walk far enough in any direction and you return to where you started. This is not metaphor. The roads loop. The horizons repeat. Travelers who don't know this walk for weeks convinced they are making progress. Those who figure it out stop walking for a while.

Nobody knows how long the world has been this way or who made it so. The people who live here — farmers, soldiers, wandering traders — have mostly stopped asking. They plant crops, fight over crossroads, die in swamps. The world turns. It always has.

You are not from here, or you have forgotten that you are. Either way you know, with a certainty you cannot explain, that there is a way out. A gate. Possibly three. They are guarded not by choice but by nature — the gate will not open for someone who has not earned passage. What earning means differs by gate. Bronze asks little. Gold asks everything.

What lies beyond the gate is not known. The game does not answer this. Characters in the world have theories — paradise, oblivion, just another looping world one size larger. The tone does not mock these theories or confirm them. The point is leaving, not arriving.

**Tone register:** folk mythology, not high fantasy. The world is worn and practical and slightly strange. Place names sound like they were given by farmers — *The Old Insistence*, *Ember Cross*, *The Stemming* — and have outlasted their original meaning. Nobody speaks in proclamations. The gate does not glow with divine light. It is just a gate that was not there yesterday.

**The Locksmith of the Unbound** is the figure who can make the key. They are not a villain or a guardian in any heroic sense. They will make it for something — food, gold. They made keys before. Most people who leave the world don't make it to the gate, or make it there unprepared, or lose the key, or turn back. The Locksmith isn't selective — they'll forge for anyone who can pay. You're just the current one. Their price is fair.

**What the player is:** not a hero. Not chosen. Just someone who figured out the world loops and decided that was unacceptable. The world has seen this before. It will see it again. This time might be different.

**What the game respects:** the player's intelligence. Nothing is explained that can be discovered. Nothing is dramatic that should be quiet. The gate opening is not a fanfare — it is a door, and you walk through it, and the world behind you keeps turning without you.
