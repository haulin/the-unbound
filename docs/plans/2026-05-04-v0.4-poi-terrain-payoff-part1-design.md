# v0.4 (Part 1) — PoI Rework & Terrain Payoff (Design)

## Context

**Prompt:** `/start v0.4 … Also - we're doing only part 1 … mark part 2 as v0.5 and shift all future milestones by 0.1`

**Key inputs from chat (brainstorm decisions):**

- Lakes become **placed PoIs** (not base terrain): **6** per map, **1–3 food** on visit, **cooldown 3** (per lake).
- Rainbow’s End becomes **placed PoIs** (not base terrain): **2** per map, **min distance 7** between them, **+30 gold** once per end (one-time per run).
- Town balance: town food should **never** be **3f/3g** → set **min price to 5g for 3 food**.
- Tame Beast: sold at farms, **10–15g**, **one per run**, increases carry cap by **+50**.
- Locksmith: modal; key costs **10 food OR 20g** (still one-time per run).

**Reasoning:** Lakes/rainbows as placed features keep terrain frequency from dominating balance. Keeping them as **feature kinds** also ensures **teleports never land on them** (teleports only pick non-feature terrain).

---

## Overview

v0.4 (Part 1) makes key PoIs feel like “beats”:

- **Farms** become a modal shop: buy food or buy a one-per-run beast that expands carry capacity.
- **Locksmith** becomes a modal exchange: pay in gold or food, then you’re done for the run.
- **Fishing Lakes** are placed payoff tiles that refill a small amount of food on a cooldown.
- **Rainbow’s End** is a placed payoff tile that grants a large one-time gold payout.
- Add a **food carry cap** so “buying food” is meaningful (and so Beast has a purpose beyond pure accumulation).

Tone constraints (from `docs/lore-and-tone.md`): folk-myth register, practical/worn, quiet presentation, no tutorial voice.

## Mechanics / design details

### New cell kinds (feature-only)

Introduce new **feature kinds** (not terrain):

- `fishingLake` — uses lake sprite, stores `id` and `nextReadyStep`.
- `rainbowEnd` — uses rainbow sprite, stores `id` and “spent” state for the run.

We will remove `lake` and `rainbow` from the base `TERRAIN_KINDS` distribution so they do not appear as regular terrain.

### Base terrain distribution (mitigation)

Removing 2 of 7 noise buckets would otherwise increase hazard terrain frequency (mountain/swamp/woods). To keep overall feel stable, we keep **7 buckets** by mapping the removed buckets to safe terrain:

- Replace the old `lake` bucket with `grass`.
- Replace the old `rainbow` bucket with `road`.

Resulting distribution stays “7-way” while ensuring **mountain/swamp/woods** remain at roughly their previous frequency.

### Placement rules

- Place **6** `fishingLake` cells on terrain.
- Place **2** `rainbowEnd` cells on terrain.
- Enforce rainbow-end separation: **min distance 7** (shortest wrapping distance), clamped to map maximum like Gate/Locksmith.
- No minimum distance between lakes (explicitly accepted; we’ll tune after playtests).

### Food carry cap

- Carry cap = \(2 × armySize\).
- If tame beast owned, cap += 50.
- **Player learning rule:** buying food while already at max carry should show an explicit “cannot carry more” message and should **not** execute the trade.
- Other food gains (lake fishing, farm harvest/other events) may clamp silently; the “cannot carry more” message on purchase is considered sufficient teaching.

### Economy knobs (initial)

- **Farm food**: 3 food for 3 gold (repeatable; no cooldown; bounded by carry cap).
- **Town food**: 3 food costs **≥ 5 gold** (raise `TOWN_PRICE_FOOD_MIN` accordingly).
- **Locksmith key**: 10 food OR 20 gold.
- **Rainbow end**: +30 gold once.
- **Fishing lake**: +1..3 food when ready; cooldown 3.
- **Tame beast**: 10–15 gold; one per run; +50 cap.

### Modal encounters: button/sprite mapping (proposal)

Farms and Locksmith will follow the Town convention: **East is Leave** (`return`).

Sprite decisions from brainstorm (**explicit IDs; no placeholders** — update `src/core/spriteIds.ts` accordingly during implementation):

- **Buy Beast icon:** `#132` (donkey).
- **Farm encounter illustration:** `#162` (barn).
- **Locksmith encounter illustration:** `#194` (kiln).
- **Pay Gold button icon:** `#98` (repurpose the current “return” button sprite as “gold”).
- **Leave/Exit button icon:** `#108` (move the current “exit/return” button to `#108`).
- **Buy/Give Food button icon:** `#130` (food button).

### Mechanics integration (existing seams)

This slice should be implemented as mechanic modules, consistent with `docs/the-unbound-learnings.md` (“mechanics live in mechanic modules”):

- Introduce two new mechanic defs:
  - `src/core/mechanics/defs/fishingLake.ts` for `fishingLake` (onEnter, cooldown, +1..3 food).
  - `src/core/mechanics/defs/rainbowEnd.ts` for `rainbowEnd` (onEnter, +30 gold once).
- Change farms and locksmith from “onEnter does the whole thing” into modal encounters:
  - `farm` becomes an encounter (`startEncounter` + right-grid offers + explicit leave action).
  - `locksmith` becomes an encounter (`startEncounter` + right-grid offers + explicit leave action).

Under current architecture:

- “Modal” means `state.encounter != null`, so `ACTION_MOVE` is ignored until a leave action ends the encounter.
- Encounter-specific actions should live in a small focused module (as `town.ts` does today), so the reducer stays an orchestrator.

### Map / signposts / scout (explicit decisions)

- **Map labels:** show `rainbowEnd` on the map **only when mapped/encountered**, with label `R`.
- **Fishing lakes:** do not appear on the map (no map label).
- **Scout global reveal:** unchanged (Scout still globally reveals only `farm/camp/henge/town` when oriented).
- **Signposts:** do **not** include `fishingLake` or `rainbowEnd` as candidates (signposts remain focused on gate/locksmith + core named PoIs).

## Behavioral Impact

- **Modified behaviors:**
  - Worldgen no longer produces `lake` / `rainbow` as base terrain; they become explicit feature placements.
  - Town food price floor increases (no more 3f/3g).
  - Farms and Locksmith become modal encounters (MOVE blocked until Leave).
  - Food is capped by carry capacity.
- **New behaviors:**
  - Fishing lake cooldown payout.
  - Rainbow end one-time gold payout.
  - Tame beast carry-cap upgrade.
- **Existing tests requiring updates:**
  - `tests/core/v0.1-lost.acceptance.test.ts` (teleport landing terrain-kind allowlist currently includes `lake`/`rainbow`).
  - Any worldgen/map tests that assume terrain buckets include 7 kinds.
