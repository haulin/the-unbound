# v0.5 — The Wyrm (Design)

## Context

**Prompt:** `/grill-me` on the lore and milestone reshuffle needed to give the run a middle act before the demo (v0.8+).

**State before this design:**

- Win condition: find Locksmith (pay gold or food for key) → find Gate (opens with key).
- `docs/lore-and-tone.md` established: torus world, folk-mythology register, Locksmith as fair-priced craftsman, three gates (bronze, silver, gold) with bronze *"asking little"* and gold *"asking everything."*
- Locksmith / Gate / Bronze Key system defined in `docs/plans/2026-04-30-v0.0.9-the-key-design.md`.
- Previous roadmap: v0.5 Camps/Terrain/Henge → v0.6 Random Encounters → v0.7 Taverns → v0.8 Sanctuary & Special Items (demo target).

**Problems:**

- *"Find 2 tiles"* is not enough of a journey. The run lacked a middle act.
- The Locksmith's diegetic weight was thin — the lore had to hand-wave why others hadn't succeeded.
- The Gate was anticlimactic — arriving with 0 food and 1 soldier still won.
- Pegasus (planned for v0.8 Sanctuary) had an unsolved 4-button destination-selection UI problem; Sanctuary content was generally underspecified.

## Design decisions

Decisions are listed in dependency order. Each one closed off branches that would otherwise change downstream answers.

### 1. Why is the ingredient renewable across runs?

Considered:

- **(A)** The creature doesn't truly die — it returns, like the looping roads.
- **(B)** The creature is bled, not killed — it survives and recovers; the ingredient is produced (blood, scale, egg).
- **(C)** Each map seed has its own creature — lampshades the roguelike structure.

**Chosen: (B), with non-lethal combat resolution.** The wyrm is weakened enough to draw blood and crawls back to its lair to recover. Solves the renewability problem without metaphysics about death-and-return. No death animation needed.

### 2. What kind of creature?

Considered:

- **Wizard** (sprite already drawn, no current role). Transactional, not combat. Asset reuse. Strong folk-myth fit.
- **Dragon / wyrm / wyvern** (sprite already drawn). Combat encounter. Strong folk-myth fit if framed as wyrm rather than heraldic dragon.
- **Goblin chief.** Named combat variant — but conflates with the planned generic goblin combat variety.

**Chosen: dragon / wyrm.** Combat is the most enjoyable system in the game; the middle act should use it. The user's combat-balance instinct and the established Pay-button pattern both point at a fight here. Wizard sprite parked for higher gates or a tavern variant later.

### 3. Naming — wyrm, dragon, or wyvern?

The lore tone is explicitly folk-mythology, not high fantasy. *"Wyrm"* is the older English word and predates heraldic dragons; *"dragon"* is universally recognisable but Tolkien-coded; *"wyvern"* is specifically heraldic. The existing sprite (no legs, serpent body, two wings) is ambiguous enough that folk myth would call it any of them.

**Chosen: multiple terms by register.** `WYRM_NAME = 'The Wyrm'` as the canonical narrator term; `BARKEEP_TIPS.wyrm` says *"dragon"* explicitly for clarity; lore strings mix freely. Mirrors the existing *"donkey sprite, never named 'tame beast' directly"* pattern.

### 4. What is the ingredient?

Considered:

- **The Ember** (a coal that does not cool). Folk-myth perfect but the sprite is hard — every "ember" icon reads as generic flame, and acquisition (you scooped a hot coal from the rubble) is wobbly.
- **A scale or tooth.** Concrete, but loses the thematic tie to fire.
- **A vial of blood.** Concrete, trivial 12×12 sprite, single recoloured silhouette across future tiers (red for bronze, paler for silver, stranger for gold).
- **An egg.** Weird unless the locksmith does something specific with it.

**Chosen: the Blood, used as a quench by the Locksmith.** Pre-heraldic folk myth — *"quenched in dragon's blood"* is Beowulf / Siegfried tier. Sprite is trivial (corked vial of red). Acquisition is clear (the wyrm bled, you took some). Renewability is intrinsic (while the wyrm lives, blood can be drawn). Naming is flexible across registers: *"the Blood"* in narrator voice, *"dragon's blood"* in barkeep voice, *"the quench"* in Locksmith dialog.

### 5. Does the Locksmith change?

Considered:

- **Replace gold/food entirely with the Blood.** Loses the existing payment choice.
- **The Blood as an alternative to gold/food.** Defeats the point of the middle act (rich players skip it).
- **Additive.** Locksmith still wants gold OR food AND requires the Blood.

**Chosen: additive.** Smallest implementation change, preserves the existing Locksmith encounter shape, and reinforces the lore — *the Locksmith is fair-priced; the Blood is just what the forge has always needed.* The existing *"Their price is fair"* line in `docs/lore-and-tone.md:15` survives unchanged.

### 6. Does the Gate change?

Considered:

- **Add gate guards.** Contradicts `docs/lore-and-tone.md:9` (*"guarded not by choice but by nature"*) and dilutes the wyrm as the climax. Would also undermine the lore's explicit quiet-ending principle (*"The gate opening is not a fanfare"*).
- **Multi-criteria gate test** (*"the gate measures you"* — accepts key OR army threshold OR gold threshold). Strong hook for multiple win conditions.
- **No change.**

**Chosen: no change for the demo.** The wyrm IS the climax; the gate is the falling action. Multi-criteria gate test deferred to v0.9+ where silver/gold gates need an escalation pattern.

### 7. Where does the Wyrm live?

Considered: mountains, swamps, woods, unique terrain (caldera).

**Chosen: a cave-tile that replaces a mountain tile in a mountain cluster.** No new placement category — just a constraint on existing PoI placement. Existing mountain lore (*"The wind here forgets to carry sound"* in `src/core/lore.ts`) retroactively reads as foreshadowing. *Cave* sprite preferred over *caldera* for 12×12 legibility; caldera reserved for a possible higher-tier lair later.

### 8. How does the player discover the Wyrm?

**Chosen: all four channels, layered.**

1. **Locksmith first-visit (without Blood)** — inline flavor hints at the quench. Implicit; doesn't reveal location.
2. **Signposts** — Lair becomes a valid signpost target alongside Locksmith and Gate. Reuses existing mechanic.
3. **Barkeep tips** — explicit, literal-register rumours about *"a dragon in the high passes."* Lives in the existing town tip pool (no need to wait for v0.8 taverns).
4. **First-visit Lair lore** — contextual teaching on arrival.

Different channels serve different player types (lore-readers, signpost-followers, rumour-buyers, walk-in-blind explorers).

### 9. Win-condition narrative — static or dynamic?

Considered:

- **Static** — `GOAL_NARRATIVE` shown once at start, no on-screen tracker.
- **Static + minimal goal-tracker UI** — adds a *"Goal: Key + Blood"* element. Out-of-tone (feels like a quest log).
- **Dynamic** — `GOAL_NARRATIVE` rewrites itself as the player learns. The narrative IS the tracker.

**Chosen: static for the demo.** Replayability beats hand-holding — the whole arc shouldn't reveal itself on run 1. Dynamic narrative deferred to post-demo, when most players have multiple runs under their belt.

### 10. Roadmap placement

The Wyrm arc is the *only* change that makes the demo cohere. Original recommendation was v0.8 (replacing Sanctuary), revised to v0.6 (insert + shift), then revised again to v0.5 after dependency objections were dismantled:

- Mountain clustering already works in worldgen — not a feature to implement.
- The Pay button pattern can debut here (the Wyrm) and be reused by brigand recruit later, with no rework cost.
- Old v0.5 was overloaded (four loose themes); splitting it gives the Wyrm a focused milestone.

**Chosen:** Wyrm = v0.5; old v0.5 contents shift to v0.6; old v0.6 → v0.7; old v0.7 → v0.8 (demo release); old v0.8 Sanctuary scrapped (Pegasus moved to deferred wishlist; Tame Beast stays at farms).

### 11. Locksmith UI when the player has no Blood

Considered:

- **(A)** Modal opens with food/gold buttons enabled, lore warns the key won't work — confusing enabled-but-broken UX.
- **(B)** Let the player forge an "ordinary key" that doesn't open the gate — punishing (triple journey) but lore-rich.
- **(C)** Modal opens with only Leave + the no-Blood lore lines — useless modal to dismiss.
- **(D)** Non-modal — walking onto the tile shows inline flavor only, no modal opens. Matches the planned *"skip modal if nothing to do"* pattern from the Ideas backlog.

**Chosen: (D).** Reuses a UX pattern that's already wanted for camps/farms/towns; designing it once and applying everywhere is cleaner. Option (B) is preserved in the *After demo wishlist* as a possible hard-mode behaviour for repeat players.

## Resulting design (what the v0.5 player experiences)

1. Run starts. `GOAL_NARRATIVE` mentions a bronze gate that breaks the loop. (Unchanged from current.)
2. Player explores. Walks onto the Locksmith tile without the Blood — sees inline flavor only (no modal): *"The forge has heat enough. What it lacks is the quench."*
3. Player learns about the wyrm via signposts, barkeep rumours, or first-visit Lair lore.
4. Player travels to the Lair (cave tile in a mountain cluster). Modal combat encounter: **Fight / Pay / Flee.**
5. Player defeats the wyrm (combat ends when blood is drawn — the wyrm survives) OR pays gold (bribes for the right to bleed it). Either way: gains the Blood.
6. Player returns to the Locksmith. With Blood + gold/food payment, the modal opens normally — buys the bronze key. Purchase lore now includes a quench beat (*"The blade plunges into the vial. The hiss is brief. The key is bronze and done."*).
7. Player travels to the Gate. Uses key. Wins.

Bronze now genuinely *"asks little"* (`docs/lore-and-tone.md:9`) only relative to silver and gold — there is a real journey behind it.

## Deferred (post-demo)

- Pegasus / fast-travel — needs a 4-button destination-selection UI solution.
- Multi-criteria gate test for silver/gold gates (*"the gate measures you"*).
- Dynamic `GOAL_NARRATIVE` that rewrites itself as the player learns.
- Cross-run memory — persistent flags for *"you bled the Wyrm in a prior run"* and similar.
- Ordinary key as a learnable mistake — hard-mode behaviour.
- Wizard sprite — held for higher gates or a tavern variant.
